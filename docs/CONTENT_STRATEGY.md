# Content Strategy

## Course Taxonomy
Courses are organized into the following tracks:
1. AI Foundations for Nursing Students
2. AI for Postgraduate Nursing and Research
3. AI for Working Nurses and Clinical Productivity
4. AI for Nursing Faculty and Educators
5. Advanced AI, Projects, Mentoring, and Publication Support

## Learner Personas
- **The Student:** Needs basic AI literacy, exam prep assistance, and safe study habits.
- **The Educator (Faculty):** Needs lesson planning tools, rubric generators, and policy guidelines.
- **The Working Nurse:** Needs documentation productivity, patient education simplification, and career growth.
- **The Researcher/Postgrad:** Needs literature search strategies, proposal formatting, and mentor matching.

## Curriculum Mapping
- Content must align with Indian Nursing Council (INC) frameworks.
- Do not copy INC materials verbatim; summarize and provide original pedagogical content.
- Use Bloom's Taxonomy for learning objectives.

## Bilingual Content Workflow
- Content managed with English and Hindi fields (`title_en`, `title_hi`, `body_en`, `body_hi`).
- Translating complex medical/AI terminology into accessible Hindi suitable for nursing students (avoid overly Sanskritized or machine-like outputs).
- Glossary available for key terms.

## Assessment Design
- Questions mapped to Bloom's Taxonomy levels.
- Case-based scenarios focused on practical application.
- Faculty must review AI-generated drafts.

## Media and Reference Policy
- Strict licensing adherence (Public Domain, CC0, CC BY, or owned content).
- Every image requires alt text.
- Every video requires a transcript or captions.
- References must be authentic and properly formatted (e.g., Vancouver/APA as per context).
